<p><p>Separation systems in commercial biotechnology encompass various techniques for isolating biomolecules. Microarrays enable high-throughput analysis of DNA or proteins on a solid surface. Lab-on-a-chip integrates multiple laboratory processes on a single chip for efficient analysis. Biochips, similar to microarrays, allow for simultaneous measurement of biomolecules. Magnetic separation utilizes magnetic fields to isolate target particles. Chromatography separates substances based on their movement through a medium. Flow cytometry analyzes cell characteristics, while membrane filtration and electrophoresis separate based on size and charge, respectively. Centrifugation uses centrifugal force for separation, with other emerging methods evolving in the field.</p></p>
