<p><p>The Aziridine crosslinker market is segmented into difunctional and trifunctional groups. Difunctional aziridines possess two reactive sites, enabling them to form crosslinks between polymers, enhancing material strength and stability. Trifunctional aziridines contain three reactive sites, allowing for a more robust crosslinking network, resulting in increased rigidity and thermal resistance. These crosslinkers are used across various industries, including adhesives, coatings, and polymers, where improved mechanical properties and durability are essential for performance and longevity.</p></p>
