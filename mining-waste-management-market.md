<p><p>Mining waste management encompasses several market types, including waste rock, tailings, and mining water. Waste rock refers to the non-valuable material removed during mining operations, often stored in piles. Tailings are the residual materials left after the extraction of valuable minerals, typically comprising fine particles that require careful management to prevent environmental contamination. Mining water includes the water used for mineral extraction, processing, and dust suppression, which must be treated and managed to minimize ecological impact and comply with regulatory standards.</p></p>
