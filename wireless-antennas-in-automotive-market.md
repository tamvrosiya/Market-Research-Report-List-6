<p><p>Wireless antennas in the automotive market are crucial for communication and navigation systems. UHF (Ultra High Frequency) antennas typically operate between 300 MHz and 3 GHz, enabling efficient transmission of data, voice, and video signals, which support advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and vehicle-to-everything (V2X) communication. In contrast, VHF (Very High Frequency) antennas work between 30 MHz and 300 MHz, primarily used for radio communications and traditional broadcasting. Both types enhance connectivity and contribute to the overall advancement of automotive technology.</p></p>
