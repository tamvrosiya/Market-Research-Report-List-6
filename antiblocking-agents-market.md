<p><ul><li>Organic</li><li>Inorganic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The antiblocking agents market is divided into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic antiblocking agents are typically derived from natural sources or synthetic polymers, enhancing the slip and anti-sticking properties in films and coatings. In contrast, inorganic antiblocking agents consist of mineral-based materials like silica or talc, which help improve surface properties without affecting transparency. Both types serve to minimize surface adhesion, facilitating easier handling and processing in various applications such as packaging and plastics.</p></p>
