<p><p>The steel coil market primarily comprises hot-rolled and cold-rolled steel coils. Hot-rolled coil steel is produced by rolling heated steel above its recrystallization temperature, providing strength and malleability, often used in construction and automotive applications. In contrast, cold-rolled steel coil undergoes further processing at room temperature, resulting in a smoother finish and tighter tolerances, making it ideal for precision applications like appliances and furniture. Both types play crucial roles in various industries, reflecting differing manufacturing processes and end-use requirements.</p></p>
