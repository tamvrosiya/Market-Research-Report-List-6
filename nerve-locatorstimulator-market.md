<p><p>The nerve locator/stimulator market comprises two primary types: fixed and variable nerve locators/stimulators. Fixed nerve locators are designed with a set stimulation frequency, providing consistent performance for specific procedures. In contrast, variable nerve locators offer adjustable stimulation settings, allowing clinicians to customize nerve response based on individual patient anatomy and requirements. Both types are essential for enhancing surgical precision, minimizing nerve damage, and improving patient outcomes during procedures that require nerve identification and monitoring.</p></p>
