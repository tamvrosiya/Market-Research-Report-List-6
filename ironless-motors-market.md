<p><p>Ironless motors, designed to minimize weight and enhance efficiency, are categorized into two primary types: DC and AC. DC ironless motors utilize direct current to generate motion, characterized by a simple design and excellent torque characteristics, making them suitable for applications requiring precise control. In contrast, AC ironless motors operate with alternating current and are commonly used in applications where power efficiency and continuous operation are essential. Both types cater to diverse industries, including robotics, aerospace, and automation.</p></p>
