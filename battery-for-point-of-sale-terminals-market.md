<p><p>The market for batteries used in Point of Sale (POS) terminals includes several types: lead-acid, lithium-ion (Li-Ion), and nickel-cadmium (Ni-Cd) batteries. Lead-acid batteries are cost-effective but heavier, making them less suitable for portable devices. Li-Ion batteries offer high energy density, lightweight, and longer lifespans, ideal for modern POS applications. Ni-Cd batteries provide robust performance under extreme conditions but suffer from memory effects, reducing their capacity over time. Each type serves specific needs in the POS terminal ecosystem.</p></p>
