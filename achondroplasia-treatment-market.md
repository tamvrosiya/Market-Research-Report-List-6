<p><p>The Achondroplasia treatment market includes various therapeutic approaches. CNP analogues mimic natural C-type natriuretic peptide, promoting bone growth by stimulating cartilage development. Ligand traps inhibit overactive fibroblast growth factor receptor 3 (FGFR3) signaling, which restricts bone growth in Achondroplasia. Tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs) target FGFR3 pathways to reduce its inhibitory effects on bone growth. Other treatments may involve growth hormone therapy or surgical interventions. These therapies aim to address the underlying causes of Achondroplasia, improving height and quality of life for affected individuals.</p></p>
