<p><strong>The Distributed Fibre Optic Sensor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Rayleigh Scattering Based Distributed Sensor</li><li>Brillouin Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Raman Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Interferometric Distributed Optical-Fibre Sensor</li><li>Distributed Fibre Bragg Grating Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Distributed Fiber Optic Sensor market encompasses various technologies: Rayleigh scattering-based sensors utilize the scattering of light to measure temperature and strain along the fiber. Brillouin scattering-based sensors leverage frequency shifts for long-range measurements. Raman scattering-based sensors detect temperature variations through inelastic scattering. Interferometric distributed optical-fiber sensors use phase shifts to analyze disturbances. Finally, Distributed Fiber Bragg Grating sensors employ periodic refractive index variations to reflect specific wavelengths, enabling precise measurement of strain and temperature, making them vital for structural monitoring.</p></p>
