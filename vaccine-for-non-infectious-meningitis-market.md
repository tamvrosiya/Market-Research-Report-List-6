<p><p>The vaccine market for non-infectious meningitis encompasses several types, each targeting different strains. Polysaccharide vaccines use purified polysaccharides from bacterial capsules to elicit an immune response, while conjugate vaccines link these polysaccharides to proteins, enhancing effectiveness, particularly in young children. Combination vaccines integrate multiple antigens for broader protection with fewer shots. Men B vaccines specifically target group B meningococcal strains, addressing a significant cause of bacterial meningitis. Each type serves distinct populations and enhances overall public health efforts.</p></p>
