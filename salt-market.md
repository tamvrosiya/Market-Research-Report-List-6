<p><p>Salt market types are categorized based on their sources. Sea salt is harvested through evaporating seawater, known for its coarse texture and mineral content. Well mineral salt is extracted from underground salt deposits, often rich in trace minerals, and is typically used in food processing and culinary applications. Lake salt is derived from saltwater lakes, usually featuring distinctive flavors and colors. Each type serves diverse culinary, industrial, and dietary needs, reflecting varying production methods and mineral compositions.</p></p>
