<p><p>Glass substrates play a crucial role in the semiconductor market, providing essential support for electronic components. Borosilicate glass substrates are valued for their thermal and chemical resistance, making them ideal for high-temperature applications. Silicon glass substrates combine the properties of silicon and glass for enhanced performance in microelectronic devices. Ceramic glass substrates offer durability and stability, catering to high-frequency applications. Lastly, quartz-based glass substrates are known for their optical transparency and low thermal expansion, making them suitable for photonic devices.</p></p>
