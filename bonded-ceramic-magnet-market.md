<p><p>The bonded ceramic magnet market encompasses various manufacturing techniques: extruding, injection molding, and calendering. </p><p>Extruding involves shaping materials under high temperature and pressure to form continuous lengths, ideal for producing complex geometries. Injection molding allows for high-volume production of intricate designs by injecting molten material into molds, ensuring consistent quality. Calendering involves passing materials through rollers to achieve desired thickness and density, suitable for flat magnets. Each method offers distinct advantages for scalability, customization, and efficiency in magnet production.</p></p>
