<p><p>The electrical humidity sensor market primarily features two types: capacitive and resistive sensors. Capacitive humidity sensors operate by measuring changes in capacitance caused by varying humidity levels, offering high accuracy and fast response times. In contrast, resistive humidity sensors function by detecting changes in electrical resistance as moisture content alters, typically being more cost-effective but less precise. Both technologies cater to diverse applications, such as HVAC systems, industrial processes, and consumer electronics, driving innovation and efficiency in humidity measurement.</p></p>
