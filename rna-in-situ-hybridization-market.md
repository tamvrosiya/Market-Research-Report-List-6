<p><p>RNA In Situ Hybridization (ISH) encompasses several market types, including GISH (Genomic In Situ Hybridization), FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization), mFISH (multiplex FISH), and PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) applications. GISH is used for identifying genomic structures, while FISH targets specific RNA sequences using fluorescent probes for visualization. mFISH allows simultaneous detection of multiple targets, enhancing analysis complexity. PCR complements these techniques by amplifying RNA sequences prior to hybridization, increasing sensitivity and specificity of molecular detection in research and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
