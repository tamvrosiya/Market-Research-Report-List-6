<p><ul><li>Anhydrous Chlorobutanol</li><li>Hemihydrate Chlorobutanol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Chlorobutanol market includes two primary types: Anhydrous Chlorobutanol and Hemihydrate Chlorobutanol. Anhydrous Chlorobutanol is a water-free form, commonly used as a preservative, sedative, and antimicrobial agent in pharmaceuticals and cosmetics due to its stability and solubility in organic solvents. Hemihydrate Chlorobutanol contains a small amount of water, making it suitable for applications requiring controlled moisture levels, such as injectable medications. Both types are valued for their antimicrobial properties, but their specific uses depend on formulation requirements and moisture sensitivity.</p></p>
